What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation is the EU's data-protection law. It governs how organisations collect, store, process, and share personal data of EU residents.

Applies to any organisation handling EU personal data — regardless of where the organisation is based.

The seven core principles

  • Lawfulness, fairness and transparency
  • Purpose limitation
  • Data minimisation
  • Accuracy
  • Storage limitation
  • Integrity and confidentiality
  • Accountability

Your responsibilities

  • Only collect data with a clear lawful basis.
  • Tell people what you collect and why.
  • Honour subject-access and deletion requests within 30 days.
  • Report breaches to the supervisory authority within 72 hours.
